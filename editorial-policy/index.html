Editorial Policy

Pages are selected for fit, usable routes, and clear search intent separation.

DEAD STRIKE is a curated browser shooter site. A page is not published just because a game exists. The site prefers routes that fit the shooter lane, load cleanly, and deserve distinct categorization instead of duplicating nearby pages.

Editorial priorities

Clarity over volume

The site prioritizes playable quality, accurate grouping, and unique page purpose over raw page count.

Playable routes Clear categories Correction workflow

Selection

New pages are reviewed for topic fit before they enter the library.

  • The route should stay close to shooter, zombie, FPS, sniper, or multiplayer combat intent.
  • The page should have a working playable path, either embedded or via a clean direct tab.
  • The title and summary should be specific enough to avoid near-duplicate page purpose.

Categorization

Pages are grouped by the strongest player intent, not by every possible tag.

  • Broad hubs cover zombie, FPS, and shooter discovery.
  • Narrow hubs cover sniper, zombie defense, and multiplayer shooter intent.
  • Breadcrumbs and schema reflect one clear path so the page signal stays stable.

Updates

Pages are revised when embeds break, copy drifts, or metadata stops matching reality.

  • Playable routes are checked when the library expands or layout changes.
  • Structured data, canonicals, and sitemap entries are updated when support pages or hubs are added.
  • Legacy aliases may stay live as noindex support routes, but they are not treated as primary pages.

Corrections

Correction requests are evaluated against the live page and source reality.

  • Page-level factual issues can be reported through the contact page.
  • Requests should include the exact URL and the specific claim that looks wrong.
  • If a route no longer fits the site, it may be revised, recategorized, or removed from hub placement.
