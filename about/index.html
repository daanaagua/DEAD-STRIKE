About DEAD STRIKE

A browser shooter site built around playable routes, not empty shells.

DEAD STRIKE starts with a core zombie FPS page, then expands outward into curated browser game detail pages and category hubs. The goal is simple: help players reach a working shooter page fast, compare nearby options, and move between zombie, FPS, sniper, defense, and multiplayer routes without friction.

Mission

The site exists to make browser shooter discovery cleaner.

Many game pages on the web are thin wrappers with weak labeling and poor routing. DEAD STRIKE tries to do the opposite: keep the play path obvious, explain what kind of run each page offers, and connect each mission to nearby categories that make sense.

Structure

The library is organized around usable player intent.

Broad hubs handle core discovery, while narrower hubs cover sniper, zombie defense, and multiplayer shooter searches. Detail pages stay focused on one main route so players and search engines can understand why each page exists.

Zombie Games

Undead firefights, survival loops, and fast browser routes.

Sniper Games

Precision-focused pages for long-shot and overwatch intent.

Multiplayer Shooter Games

Drop-in PvP and co-op routes with live-match energy.

What gets published

Pages are chosen for fit, clarity, and live usability.

  • Browser shooter pages that align with zombie, FPS, sniper, or multiplayer combat intent.
  • Working routes that can be embedded or reached cleanly in a direct tab.
  • Pages with enough identity to deserve their own title, summary, and internal links.

Trust signals

The site now publishes its policy and editorial layer openly.

  • Contact explains the current public contact path.
  • Privacy Policy explains analytics and embedded third-party game hosts.
  • Terms covers site use and third-party content limits.
  • Editorial Policy explains how pages are selected and corrected.
