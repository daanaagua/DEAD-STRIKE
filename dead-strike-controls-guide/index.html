Controls guide

Use movement, aim, sprint, and weapon swaps as one survival rhythm.

Dead Strike uses familiar FPS controls, but it punishes static habits faster than many browser shooters do. The goal is not just to remember the keys. The goal is to chain the keys together so every reload, dodge, and weapon swap happens while you are already making space.

Core keys

The basic layout is familiar, but the pressure pattern is not.

  • `W A S D`: move and strafe.
  • `Mouse`: aim and fire.
  • `Shift`: sprint to create space before the next close-range rush.
  • `Space`: jump or dodge when the lane is about to collapse.
  • `1-6`: swap weapons before the current gun becomes a trap.

What matters most

Do not reload or swap like you are safe.

In Dead Strike, reloads and weapon changes are not neutral actions. They are risk windows. Try to reload only after you have already rotated away from the nearest pack, and switch weapons before you are cornered instead of after.

Browser setup

Use fullscreen or the direct tab when you want cleaner sightlines.

  • Open the full game tab if the embedded frame feels tight.
  • Keep the play area wide so mouse tracking feels steadier.
  • On smaller devices, the direct tab is often the safer path.

Practical habit

Think in pairs: move and aim, sprint and reload, swap and reposition.

The fastest way to improve is to stop treating each button as a separate action. Dead Strike rewards control pairs. When movement and timing stay connected, the same wave becomes much easier to manage.
